# Fake Job and Part-Time Income Scams

## Overview
Fake job scams lure victims with promises of high-income, work-from-home tasks. Typically, these involve completing simple YouTube video likes, rating hotels on travel sites, or reviewing e-commerce goods. The fraudsters request registration deposits or "processing fees" to unlock higher-tier payouts, which are never delivered.

## Warning Signs
*   **YouTube Likes Task**: Lures promising Rs. 50-100 per video like or Google maps review.
*   **Telegram Transition**: Moving chats from WhatsApp to Telegram channels showing fabricated payout screenshots.
*   **Security Deposit demands**: Requesting capital deposits to "activate" high-commission merchant jobs.

## Prevention Guidance
*   **Verify Company Details**: Check the registration of the employer via official corporate registries.
*   **No Pay-To-Work**: Authentic recruiters do not request payment for training material, laptops, or registration steps.
*   **Report Lures**: Report suspicious Telegram recruiter groups to the Cyber Cell immediately.
