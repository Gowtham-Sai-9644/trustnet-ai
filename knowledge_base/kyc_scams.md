# KYC Expiry and Account Freeze Scams

## Overview
Fake KYC (Know Your Customer) scams are social engineering luring mechanisms where fraudsters pretend to be bank officials, telecom providers, or payment gateway agents (e.g., Paytm, SBI, HDFC). They threaten the user with immediate account suspension or card freezing unless KYC details are updated.

## Warning Signs
*   **Urgent demands**: Threatening that your account will be blocked within "2 hours" or "today".
*   **Phone calls or SMS**: Requesting to install remote access apps (e.g., AnyDesk, TeamViewer) or click on short links (e.g., `bit.ly`, `tinyurl.com`).
*   **Unsolicited forms**: Asking for sensitive banking coordinates like PINs, OTPs, or credit card CVVs.

## RBI Recommendations
*   **Never share OTPs**: RBI guidelines state that no banking official will ask for passwords, CVVs, or OTPs over SMS or calls.
*   **Official Portals**: Always perform re-KYC procedures through the bank's official web domain or by visiting a branch.
*   **Report Fraud**: Dial the National Cyber Crime Helpline at `1930` or visit `cybercrime.gov.in`.
